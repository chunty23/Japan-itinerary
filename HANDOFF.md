# 🍜 TAB 2: Food Guide

**Title:** Japan 2026 — Food & Drink Guide

## ✈  Kagoshima & Fukuoka

### Kurobuta Pork (any yakiniku spot) (Kagoshima)
- **Category:** Yakiniku
- **Price/person:** $10–30
- **Must-try dish:** Berkshire pork belly & ribs
- **Notes:** Kagoshima's most prized ingredient — look for 黒豚 on any grill menu
- **Reservation needed?** No

### Kibinago Ryori (Kagoshima)
- **Category:** Sashimi
- **Price/person:** $10–30
- **Must-try dish:** Kibinago (silver herring) sashimi or tempura
- **Notes:** Local delicacy only available fresh in Kagoshima
- **Reservation needed?** No

### Tenmonkan area izakayas (Kagoshima)
- **Category:** Izakaya
- **Price/person:** $10–30
- **Must-try dish:** Shochu tasting flights
- **Notes:** Try local Imo-jochu (sweet potato shochu) — very different from sake
- **Reservation needed?** No

### Ichiran (Hakata origin) (Fukuoka)
- **Category:** Ramen
- **Price/person:** $1–10
- **Must-try dish:** Tonkotsu ramen
- **Notes:** This IS the home of Ichiran — tastes better here. Solo booths, order on form
- **Reservation needed?** No

### Nakasu Yatai stalls (Fukuoka)
- **Category:** Street Food
- **Price/person:** $10–30
- **Must-try dish:** Yakitori, ramen, oden
- **Notes:** Riverside food stalls open from dusk — a Fukuoka institution
- **Reservation needed?** No


## ✈  Kumamoto

### Basashi (horse sashimi) — any local izakaya (Kumamoto)
- **Category:** Sashimi
- **Price/person:** $10–30
- **Must-try dish:** Horse sashimi with ginger & garlic
- **Notes:** Kumamoto's most famous dish — mild, lean, surprisingly delicious
- **Reservation needed?** No

### Ikinari Dango shops (Kumamoto)
- **Category:** Snack
- **Price/person:** $1–10
- **Must-try dish:** Sweet potato & anko dumpling
- **Notes:** Look for freshly steamed at markets near the castle
- **Reservation needed?** No

### Karashi Renkon stalls (Kumamoto)
- **Category:** Snack
- **Price/person:** $1–10
- **Must-try dish:** Mustard-miso stuffed lotus root
- **Notes:** Iconic local snack — packaged versions make great souvenirs
- **Reservation needed?** No

### Kumamoto Ramen (local tonkotsu shops) (Kumamoto)
- **Category:** Ramen
- **Price/person:** $1–10
- **Must-try dish:** Garlic oil tonkotsu
- **Notes:** Lighter than Hakata-style, distinct garlic oil topping
- **Reservation needed?** No


## ✈  Hiroshima

### Okonomimura (Food building, 5-13 Shintenchi) (Hiroshima)
- **Category:** Okonomiyaki
- **Price/person:** $10–30

# 💡 TAB 5: Practical Tips & Notes

**Title:** Japan 2026 — Practical Tips

**Header columns:** Category | Tip


## ✈  💴 Money & Payments

- **Money:** Japan is still largely cash-based — carry ¥10,000–¥20,000 per person per day. Many restaurants, shrines, and small shops are cash only
- **Money:** Get yen before you go or at the airport 7-Eleven ATMs (most reliable for foreign cards). Avoid currency exchange booths
- **Money:** Need a coin purse — vending machines, shrines, and small shops use lots of coins (¥100 and ¥500 especially)
- **Money:** IC cards (Suica or Pasmo) work on trains, buses, and even convenience stores — load one at the airport on arrival. Apple/Android Pay works too
- **Money:** Avoid shops with English signs and 'Tax Free' banners — prices are usually marked up. Locals don't shop there

## ✈  🚆 Transportation

- **Transport:** Get an IC card (Suica) at the airport immediately — tap on/off trains and buses everywhere, no ticket buying needed
- **Transport:** Shinkansen: Sit on the LEFT side Kyoto→Tokyo (traveling northeast — Fuji is on your left). Sit on the RIGHT side Tokyo→Kyoto (return). Nozomi is fastest. Green Car = first class.
- **Transport:** Google Maps set to 'wheelchair accessible' routes will navigate you to elevators — great for luggage without stairs
- **Transport:** Luggage forwarding (takuhaibin): Ship bags between hotels with services like Yamato Transport. ~¥1,500–2,500 per bag. Liberating for travel days
- **Transport:** Taxis in Japan are expensive but impeccable — doors open automatically, white gloves, no tipping needed (tipping is actually rude)

## ✈  🏨 Hotels & Ryokans

- **Hotels:** At a ryokan: wear the yukata (robe) provided everywhere in the hotel. Onsen etiquette: wash thoroughly before entering, no swimwear, no tattoos at most (call ahead)
- **Hotels:** Ryokan kaiseki dinners are usually served in your room or a private dining area — don't rush, it's a multi-hour experience
- **Hotels:** Luggage: store it at stations using coin lockers if checking out but not leaving the city until later. Most major stations have large lockers

## ✈  🍽️ Food & Dining

- **Food:** Many restaurants open for lunch 11am–2pm, close, then reopen for dinner 5:30–6pm. Avoid arriving between 2–5pm
- **Food:** Most restaurants require removing shoes — look for a step-up (agari) at the entrance as the cue
- **Food:** Ask for 'Nihongo no menyu onegaishimasu' (日本語のメニューお願いします) — the Japanese menu often has more items and better prices than the English tourist menu
- **Food:** Plastic food displays in windows are accurate — if you don't know what to order, point at the display
- **Food:** Conveyor belt sushi (kaiten sushi): color of plate = price. Stack your plates to be tallied at end
- **Food:** Convenience stores (konbini) are excellent: 7-Eleven, FamilyMart, Lawson have hot foods, onigiri, sandwiches, great coffee — don't skip them

## ✈  🗺️ Etiquette & Culture

- **Etiquette:** No tipping — it can be considered rude. Service is excellent because it's a source of professional pride, not tips
- **Etiquette:** Trash cans are almost impossible to find outside convenience stores. Carry a small bag for wrappers and eat where you buy
- **Etiquette:** Public restrooms usually have no paper towels — carry a small hand towel or handkerchief
- **Etiquette:** Queue everything — lines are orderly and respected. Don't cut queues or board trains before people exit
- **Etiquette:** Keep your voice low in public spaces, trains, and restaurants. Avoid phone calls on trains
- **Etiquette:** Bow when someone bows to you — a small nod is fine for foreigners
- **Etiquette:** Tattoos: public onsens often prohibit them. Private onsens (like at ryokans) are usually fine. Call ahead if unsure

## ✈  📱 Tech & Connectivity

- **Tech:** Get a pocket WiFi or SIM card at the airport — essential. IIJmio or Mobal SIM cards work well for international visitors
- **Tech:** Japan uses Type A outlets (standard US 2-prong) — no adapter needed for US travelers. Voltage is fine too
- **Tech:** Google Maps is excellent in Japan — use it for transit directions, it handles trains and buses perfectly
- **Tech:** Some shrines (like Fushimi Inari) are open 24/7 — check which ones for night visits, which are often more atmospheric and crowd-free

## ✈  🌸 Late May / Early June Specifics

- **Seasonal:** No cherry blossoms in late May — but wisteria, azaleas, and fresh green 'Midori' season is gorgeous instead
- **Seasonal:** Weather: warm and humid in late May, can get rainy in early June (start of rainy season). Pack a compact umbrella
- **Seasonal:** Crowds are lower than cherry blossom season (April) — one of the best times to visit popular sites
- **Seasonal:** Some shrines host late-spring festivals (matsuri) in late May — check the local calendar for your specific dates

**Edo-Tokyo Museum** (Sumida)
- Price: ¥400
- Major historical museum on the city's transition from Edo period to modern Tokyo. Very immersive and educational.

**Sumida Hokusai Museum** (Sumida)
- Price: ¥400
- Dedicated to ukiyo-e artist Hokusai — creator of The Great Wave off Kanagawa.


### › Shinagawa

**Tennozu Isle 'City of Art'** ⭐ (Shinagawa)
- Price: Free
- Waterfront district with repurposed warehouses, street art, creative spaces, 15+ art galleries in 2 Terrada Art Complex buildings. Great for an afternoon arts walk.

**Oi Racecourse Flea Market** ⭐ (Shinagawa)
- Price: Free entry
- Tokyo's largest weekend market — 300+ vendors, food trucks, rare collectibles, clothing, antiques. Watch out for counterfeits (laws don't apply here).

**Sengaku-ji Temple** (Shinagawa)
- Price: ¥500 museum
- Famous for the graveyard of the '47 Ronin' — the masterless samurai who avenged their master. Small but moving museum.

**Pigment Tokyo** (Shinagawa)
- Price: Free entry
- Store offering 4,500 paint pigment colors and rare art supplies — stunning to look at even without buying.

**Togoshi Ginza** (Shinagawa)
- Price: Free
- Tokyo's longest shopping street (1.3km) — 400 shops, used furniture, pharmacies, izakaya, cafes. Authentic local atmosphere.


### › Toshima (Ikebukuro)

**Pokémon Center Mega Tokyo** (Toshima)
- Price: Free entry
- One of the largest Pokémon Centers in Tokyo. Pikachu Sweets Café is takeaway only, no reservation needed.

**Sunshine City** (Toshima)
- Price: Free–¥
- Multi-building mall with shops, eateries, observation deck, aquarium, museum, and indoor theme parks. Full day destination if needed.

**Sugamo Jizodori** (Toshima)
- Price: Free
- Affectionately called 'Grandma's Harajuku' — old-style shops, flat and barrier-free streets. A glimpse of a different, quieter Tokyo.

**Animate Ikebukuro** (Toshima)
- Price: Free entry
- 9-story anime shop — one of the world's largest. Cafes, manga, art supplies.


### › The Tokyo Toilet (Shibuya area)

**The Tokyo Toilet Project** (Shibuya area)
- Price: Free
- Public art architecture project — 17 beautifully designed public restrooms by world-famous architects (Tadao Ando, Kengo Kuma, Shigeru Ban, Sou Fujimoto). Walking tour of the Yoyogi/Harajuku area. Glass toilet turns opaque when locked. Worth seeking out.


## KYOTO


### › Nishikyo & Ukyo (Arashiyama)

**Arashiyama Bamboo Grove** ⭐ (Ukyo)
- Price: Free
- For maximum effect: approach from the side gate of Tenryu-ji Temple. VERY crowded midday — go before 7am or after 6pm. Open 24/7.

**Tenryuji Temple** ⭐ (Ukyo)
- Price: ¥500
- Zen Buddhist temple — walk through main hall, explore gardens, exit via north gate which leads directly into the Bamboo Grove.

**Monkey Park Iwatayama** ⭐ (Ukyo)
- Price: ¥800
- ~120 snow monkeys. Open 9am–4pm, cash only. Steep 20-min forest hike. Short walk from Bamboo Grove. Worth the climb for monkeys AND the Kyoto views.

**Okochi Sanso Garden** ⭐ (Ukyo)
- Price: ¥1,000
- Former home of Japanese film star — traditional house, tea house, Buddhist halls, exceptional Japanese garden. Stunning and far less visited than the Bamboo Grove next door.

**Gioji Temple** ⭐ (Ukyo)
- Price: ¥300
- Beautiful moss gardens. Tragic story of dancer Gio who was betrayed — the beauty of the moss feels appropriate for the sadness of the tale.

**Otagi Nenbutsu-ji** ⭐ (Ukyo)
- Price: ¥500
- 1,200 unique and whimsical hand-carved stone statues — each one different. Far less visited than it deserves. Very atmospheric.

**Adashino Nenbutsuji** ⭐ (Ukyo)
- Price: ¥500
- 8,000 stone statues dedicated to souls who died without kin. Has its own bamboo walkway. Next to Otagi — combine the two.

**Saga-toriimoto Preserved Street** ⭐ (Ukyo)
- Price: Free
- Preserved Meiji-era street (1868–1912) lined with traditional machiya townhouses converted to shops and restaurants. Next to Adashino Nenbutsuji.

**Saihoji (Moss Temple)** (Nishikyo)
- Price: ¥40,000
- Famous for lush moss garden with 120+ moss varieties — must participate in a ritual (copying sutras) before entering. Book a time slot online. Very expensive but unique.

**Oharano Shrine** (Nishikyo)
- Price: Free
- 'Monet's Water Lily Pond' — deer are considered messengers of the gods. Covered with water lilies from early summer onwards. Very off-the-beaten-path.


### › Shimogyo & Nakagyo (Central Kyoto)

**Nishiki Market** ⭐ (Nakagyo)
- Price: Free
- 'Kyoto's Kitchen' — 100+ shops and restaurants on a long covered street. Eat street food, buy snacks and souvenirs. Closes at 6pm. Go mid-morning for best selection.

**Pontocho Alley** ⭐ (Nakagyo)
- Price: Free
- Runs parallel to the Kamo River — considered the most beautiful street in Kyoto. Traditional shops and restaurants. Visit in the evening. Some establishments are Japanese-only.

**Nijo Castle** ⭐ (Nakagyo)
- Price: ¥1,300
- Famous 'nightingale floors' that squeak as you walk — shogun's spy-proofing. Plum orchard, maple and ginkgo trees, impressive moat. Free to walk grounds.

**Teramachi & Shinkyogoku Arcades** (Nakagyo)
- Price: Free
- Two covered shopping arcades next to Nishiki Market. Teramachi has traditional long-established stores; Shinkyogoku has fashion, game centers, movie theaters for younger crowd.

**Kyoto Tower** (Shimogyo)
- Price: ¥700
- Kyoto's tallest structure — 360° viewing platform 100m up. Views of Kyoto and on clear days as far as Osaka.

**Face House** ⭐ (Nakagyo)
- Price: Free
- 1970s building designed to look like a face by architect Yamashita Kazumasa — fully functional architectural facial features. Operates as a small shop. Bizarre and wonderful.


### › Higashiyama

**Gion District** ⭐ (Higashiyama)
- Price: Free
- Traditional wooden machiya merchant houses — geisha (geiko) and maiko entertain in the ochaya teahouses. Hanami-koji Street is most popular. Shirakawa Canal lined with willows is beautiful in the evening.

**Kiyomizu-dera Temple** ⭐ (Higashiyama)
- Price: ¥500
- 'Pure Water Temple' — wooden stage 13m above hillside with city views. Otowa Waterfall at the base: three streams said to grant longevity, success at school, and fortunate love life. Don't drink all three — considered greedy.

**Ninen & Sannen-zaka Preserved Districts** ⭐ (Higashiyama)
- Price: Free
- Heian-period (794–1185) preserved lanes leading to/from Kiyomizu-dera. Most atmospheric walking in all of Kyoto. Open 10am–6pm. Extremely busy during the day.

**Sanjusangendo** ⭐ (Higashiyama)
- Price: ¥600
- 1,001 statues of Kannon (goddess of mercy) created in 1254. The math: 42 arms × 25 planes of existence = 1,000 arms. Completely unique.

**Kodaiji & Bamboo Grove** ⭐ (Higashiyama)
- Price: ¥600
- Zen Buddhist temple with multiple gardens, mausoleum, 2 tea houses. Return path passes through a bamboo grove. Less crowded than Arashiyama grove.

**Yasaka-Jinja Shrine** (Higashiyama)
- Price: Free
- Well known for July's Gion Matsuri — possibly the most famous festival in Japan. Beautiful shrine complex at the east end of Shijo Avenue.

**Jishu Shrine** (Higashiyama)
- Price: Free
- Located behind Kiyomizu-dera — dedicated to love and matchmaking. Two stones: find your way from one to the other with eyes closed = luck in love.

**Hokan-ji (Yasaka Pagoda)** (Higashiyama)
- Price: Free ext / ¥ int
- 46-meter pagoda in the middle of a historic neighborhood — the iconic Higashiyama silhouette. Very photogenic from the surrounding stone lanes.


### › Kamigyo & Sakyo

**Philosopher's Path** ⭐ (Sakyo)
- Price: Free
- 2km canal path lined with hundreds of cherry trees (beautiful in late May with fresh greenery). Restaurants and cafes along the route, plus small shrines. Connects Ginkakuji to Nanzenji.

**Nanzenji Temple** ⭐ (Sakyo)
- Price: ¥500 (Hojo/gate)
- Expansive free grounds — explore the Meiji-era aqueduct. Climb the Sanmon gate for balcony views (¥500). Konchi-in Temple with tearoom (¥500) and Tenjuan Temple (¥500) at the outskirts.

**Ginkakuji (Silver Pavilion)** (Sakyo)
- Price: ¥
- Not actually silver — named to contrast with the Golden Pavilion. Moonlight on its dark exterior gave a silver appearance. Moss garden and dry sand garden on circular walking route.

**Kyoto Botanical Gardens** (Sakyo)
- Price: ¥
- 12,000 plant species — Japan's oldest and most comprehensive public botanical garden. Beautiful in late May.

**Kifune-jinja** (Sakyo)
- Price: Free
- Incredibly photogenic stone steps lined on both sides with red lanterns. Dedicated to the god of water.

**Kyoto Imperial Palace & Kyoto Gyoen** (Kamigyo)
- Price: Free
- Residence of Japan's Imperial Family until 1868 — see palace buildings and gardens (none can be entered). Surrounding Gyoto Gyoen National Garden is the green heart of Kyoto.

**Kitano Tenmangu Shrine** (Kamigyo)
- Price: Free
- 2,000 plum trees and hundreds of maples. Flea market on the shrine grounds every 25th of the month.


### › Kita

**Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)** ⭐ (Kita)
- Price: ¥500
- Top two floors covered in gold leaf — burned down in 1950 by a fanatic monk. First floor in Heian palace style, second floor in samurai residence style. Exit area has tea garden and small matcha stall.

**Daitoku-ji** (Kita)
- Price: Free (some sub-temples ¥)
- 14th-century complex — 22 sub-temples over 56 acres. Many of Japan's best Japanese gardens are within. Some gardens only open a few weeks per year.

**Kamo Shrines (Shimogamo & Kamigamo)** (Kita)
- Price: Free
- Two of Kyoto's most important ancient shrines. Shimogamo is surrounded by the Tadasu no Mori forest with 600-year-old trees. Kamigamo features two sand purification cones.

**Funaoka Onsen** ⭐ (Kita)
- Price: ¥430
- Established 1923 — one of Kyoto's oldest bathhouses. Features historic WWII wood carvings. Authentic neighborhood sento experience (not really an onsen but priced like one).


### › Fushimi

**Fushimi Inari-Taisha** ⭐ (Fushimi)
- Price: Free
- Thousands of vermilion torii gates on trails up sacred Mt. Inari (233m, 2–3 hr full hike). Very busy until sunset — go before sunrise or after sunset. Many people only go to Yotsutsuji (30–45 min up) for the Kyoto view overlook.

**Jikkoku-bune Boats** (Fushimi)
- Price: ¥1,500
- 55-min boat drift past historic sake breweries along a cherry blossom-lined river. Capacity 20 people.


## OSAKA


### › Kita (Umeda / Tenjin)

**Umeda Sky Building** ⭐ (Kita)
- Price: ¥2,000
- Observation deck called 'Garden in the Sky' (Kuchu Teien) — stunning connected twin towers with open-air rooftop observatory.

**Tenjimbashi-suji Shopping Arcade** (Kita)
- Price: Free
- Osaka's longest (and one of Japan's longest) shopping arcades — almost endless stretch of retro shops, restaurants and cafes. Stop by Osaka Tenmangu Shrine along the way.


### › Chuo (Dotonbori / Namba)

**Dotonbori District** ⭐ (Chuo)
- Price: Free walking
- Osaka's tourist heartland — huge neon signs, clubs, bars, restaurants. Best explored after dark when the neon lights are fully on. Iconic Glico Running Man sign.

**Osaka Castle** ⭐ (Chuo)
- Price: ¥1,200 tower + ¥2,000 garden
- Impressive castle tower and stone wall museum. Nishinomaru Garden has 600 cherry trees and a tea house. Free to explore the castle grounds themselves.

**Shinsaibashi-Suji** (Chuo)
- Price: Free
- Osaka's busiest shopping arcade.

**AmeMura 'American Village'** ⭐ (Chuo)
- Price: Free
- Center of youth culture, vintage clothing, records, cafes. Live music and clubs in the evening. Osaka's answer to Shimokitazawa/Harajuku.

**Hozenji** (Chuo)
- Price: Free
- Built 1637 — Fudo Myoo statue completely covered in moss because visitors splash it with water for good luck. Nearby Hozenji Yokocho alley represents old Osaka.

**National Museum of Art** (Chuo)
- Price: ¥1,500
- Currently: Nakanishi Natsuyuki exhibit. Located on Nakanoshima-koen island in Yodo-gawa River alongside Science Museum and Oriental Ceramics Museum.


### › Naniwa & Nishi (Shinsekai)

**Tsutenkaku Tower** ⭐ (Naniwa)
- Price: ¥1,200 obs
- Retro Shinsekai district tower — indoor observatory on 5F with gold Billiken statue (stroke his feet for good luck). Rooftop outdoor deck and 'Dive & Walk' experience (harness, from 40m up).

**Spa World** ⭐ (Naniwa)
- Price: ¥2,700
- 7-story 24/7 spa with baths from around the globe — Greek, Islamic, hydrogen, carbonated, open-air. Euro Zone and Asian Zone swap gender access monthly. Ask about tattoo policy.

**Namba Yasaka Shrine** (Naniwa)
- Price: Free
- Lion-shaped stage built in 1975 — the giant mouth swallows evil spirits and brings good luck. Very unusual and photogenic shrine.

**Orange Street (Tachibana-Dori)** ⭐ (Nishi)
- Price: Free
- Fashion boutiques, craft ateliers, furniture makers, bars and cafes — described as the 'Williamsburg' of Osaka. Great afternoon browse.

**Utsubo Park** (Nishi)
- Price: Free
- One of Osaka's prettiest parks — oval rose garden, cherry trees. Best on quiet weekday afternoons.


### › Higashisumiyoshi & Sumiyoshi

**TeamLab Botanical Garden Osaka** ⭐ (Higashisumiyoshi)
- Price: ¥2,400
- Open-air permanent nighttime art installation — interactive light displays using the natural environment. Different experience from the TeamLab venues in Tokyo. Book ahead.

**Sumiyoshi Taisha** ⭐ (Sumiyoshi)
- Price: Free
- Founded in the 3rd century — one of Japan's oldest shrines in a purely Japanese style, predating Buddhist influence. Beautiful Sorihashi Bridge creates a dramatic arch over the pond.

**Miyuki-dori Koreatown** (Ikuno)
- Price: Free
- 500-meter street with 120+ Korean stores and restaurants. Great for Korean BBQ and snacks.

**Asahi Beer Museum** (Asahi)
- Price: ¥1,000
- Tour and tasting at the Suita Plant — try freshly brewed Super Dry on site.


### › Konohana

**Universal Studios Japan** (Konohana)
- Price: ¥8,900+
- First USJ outside the US. Harry Potter, Nintendo World, and more. Full day outing.

**Maishima Sports Island** (Konohana)
- Price: ¥1,500 nemophila festival
- Artificial island with a million nemophila (baby blue eyes) flowers in bloom April–early May. Also hosts Osaka edition of Summer Sonic music festival.
